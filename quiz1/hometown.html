About My Hometown


Malang City is one of the largest cities in East Java Province, Indonesia. Famous for its cool air and lush environment, Malang often becomes a favorite tourist destination for both domestic and international travelers. Not only does Malang offer natural beauty, but it also has historical and educational value that makes it a dynamic city rich in culture and social activities.

History and Origins


The city of Malang has a long history, starting from the Hindu-Buddhist kingdom era to the Dutch colonial period. During the colonial period, Malang became a favorite place for Europeans due to its cool climate and strategic location in the highlands. This is evident from the many colonial-style buildings that still stand strong to this day, such as the Malang City Hall and the Ijen Cathedral.

Education City


Malang is known as the Education City. This city has many renowned universities, including Brawijaya University, Malang State University, and Maulana Malik Ibrahim State Islamic University. The presence of various universities makes Malang a bustling city with academic and scientific activities. Many students from various regions in Indonesia come to Malang to pursue their education, creating a youthful and dynamic atmosphere in the city.

Tourist Destinations


The beauty of nature is one of the main attractions of Malang City. Situated at an altitude of between 440 and 667 meters above sea level, Malang has a cool climate year-round, making it pleasant to visit at any time. One of the famous natural tourist areas is Batu, located near the city of Malang. Batu is known as a producer of apples and has various natural tourist attractions, such as Jatim Park, the Transportation Museum, and Coban Rondo. Not only that, the mountains and hills surrounding Malang offer breathtaking views and a calming atmosphere.

Culinary and Local Culture


Malang also has a wealth of culinary delights that should not be missed. Some popular traditional foods from Malang include Bakso Malang, which is widely known throughout Indonesia, as well as Nasi Pecel Kawi and Tempe Mendol. The cuisine in Malang is not only delicious but also affordable, making it a paradise for food lovers. In addition to its cuisine, Malang is also rich in local culture. Annual events like the Malang Tempo Doeloe Festival showcase traditional arts, music performances, and unique handicrafts from Malang, providing visitors with the opportunity to experience local life and culture firsthand.
